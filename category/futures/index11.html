200 contracts / bar the sweet spot for ZG?

The average daily volume of ZG seems to be about 20,000 contracts. Given about 21 hours of trading per day. 20000 contracts / 1260 minutes \~= 15 contracts / minute. So using a minimum of 100 contracts / bar chart seems reasonable. A 200 to 300 contracts bar seem to be a good starting point on first glimpse.

The key is to maintain reaction time of the system while not overtrading. I prefer to catch the big wave and not worry about the dips and spikes too much. Ideal holding period is in hours and not minutes.

GC, YGG8, and ZGG8

GC is spot gold. YGG8 is feb'08 NYMEX gold futures. ZGG8 is feb'08 CBOT gold futures.

I thought of trading the NYMEX gold initially because it's the biggest market. However, NYMEX uses pit trading during the day and electronic at night. Whereas CBOT uses electronic exclusively. I'll be using CBOT from now on because slippage and fill time may be better, and it seems to be the favoured market for day traders. The exact reasoning is not clear to me at the moment.

Also, I'm using feb'08 because it has the largest volume at the moment. It's just not too far and not too close enough in the future to be desirable.
